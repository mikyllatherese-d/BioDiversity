Biodiversity Overview

Biodiversity comprises all living forms that we see around us. These include all essential goods and services we derive from the environment. Therefore, we can say that we depend on the richness of biodiversity for our survival. Any alteration in the biodiversity of species can affect our lives whether directly or indirectly.

Definition of Biodiversity

Biodiversity encompasses all forms of life on Earth, from bacteria to plants and animals, with an estimated 8.7 million species. It represents the richness of ecosystems and genetic diversity within species.

Role of Evolution

Evolutionary processes shape biodiversity by giving organisms traits for survival and reproduction, distinguishing them from other species.

Scientific Study of Biodiversity

Scientists study biodiversity globally, identifying hotspots of high species diversity and examining ecosystem dynamics in habitats like forests and oceans.

Ecosystem Function

Every species plays a vital role in ecosystem function, from producers capturing energy to decomposers recycling nutrients, maintaining balance and stability.

Threats to Biodiversity

Human activities such as deforestation, pollution, and climate change endanger biodiversity, increasing the risk of species extinction.

Conservation Efforts

Conservation efforts aim to protect biodiversity through measures like establishing protected areas and addressing threats like deforestation and pollution, ensuring the preservation of species and ecosystems for future generations.
