Biodiversity and Human Health

Health is our most essential human right. Most people consider health as a treasure and wealth. It means overall wellness that secures our physical, emotional, intellectual, spiritual, and social aspects. Therefore, health is a reliable indicator of the country's sustainable development. The health of people affects economic flow and growth.

Human health rests on biodiversity. The diverse collection of goods and services provides unending benefits to human health. On the contrary, the unprecedented loss of biodiversity globally imposes a threat to human health and well-being. Hence, the call to maintain healthy and sustainable biodiversity should not be disregarded. Life is impossible without a healthy and sustainable global environment.

  • Biodiversity helps secure food, health, and livelihood for people.
  • Biodiversity serves as a rich source of materials for medical research.
  • Biodiversity provides abundant resources for traditional and modern medicine.
  • Biodiversity helps in the control and prevention of infectious diseases.
  • Biodiversity serves important roles in the communities' cultural, spiritual, and social growth.
  • Biodiversity conservation is very important for climate change adaptation.
  • Stable and sustainable ecosystems support relief and recovery efforts.
